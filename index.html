Poulnabrone Dolmen

Located in the Burren, the iconic megalithic tomb at Poulnabrone dates back to the Neolithic period. There are approximately 174 of these monuments of various sizes across Ireland. However, Puolnabrone is the most widely recognized.

Blarney Castle

Blarney Castle is a medieval stronghold in Blarney, near Cork. The current keep was built in 1446 by one of Ireland's greatest chieftans - Cormac MacCarthy. The world-famous Blarney Stone is among the machicolations of the castle.

Gougane Barra

Gougane Barra (meaning 'the rock of Barra) is west of Macroom in County Cork. The name comes from Saint Finbarr. St Finbar's Oratory is the final destination for one of the five Pilgrim Paths of Ireland, as well as a popular place for weddings.
